Speed Distance Time Calculator

Calculate speed, distance or time using the universal motion formula. Select what you want to find and enter the known values.

What is the Speed Distance Time Formula?

The speed, distance and time formula is one of the most fundamental relationships in physics and everyday life. These three quantities are related by a simple equation:

Speed = Distance / Time

Distance = Speed x Time

Time = Distance / Speed

If you know any two of these values, you can always calculate the third. This calculator lets you choose which value to find and enter the other two.

How to Use This Calculator

1. Select what you want to calculate: Speed, Distance, or Time.

2. Enter the two known values in the input fields.

3. Click "Calculate" to see the result with the formula used.

4. The default units are kilometres (km) for distance, hours for time, and kilometres per hour (km/h) for speed. You can use any consistent units and interpret the result accordingly.

Unit Conversions

Here are some common conversions that may be useful:

Speed: 1 km/h = 0.2778 m/s | 1 mph = 1.609 km/h | 1 m/s = 3.6 km/h

Distance: 1 km = 1000 m | 1 mile = 1.609 km | 1 foot = 0.3048 m

Time: 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3600 seconds

Real-Life Applications

This formula is used everywhere in daily life. When planning a road trip, you can estimate travel time by dividing the distance by your average speed. Athletes use it to track their running pace. Pilots calculate flight times. Delivery services estimate arrival times. Even simple tasks like calculating how long it takes to walk to a nearby shop use this formula.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if my time is in minutes instead of hours?

Convert minutes to hours by dividing by 60. For example, 90 minutes = 90/60 = 1.5 hours. Enter 1.5 in the time field to get the correct result in km/h.

Can I use miles instead of kilometres?

Yes. If you enter distance in miles and time in hours, the speed result will be in miles per hour (mph). The formula works the same regardless of units, as long as you are consistent.

What is average speed?

Average speed is the total distance travelled divided by the total time taken. It accounts for variations in speed during the journey, such as stopping at traffic lights or slowing down on curves.
