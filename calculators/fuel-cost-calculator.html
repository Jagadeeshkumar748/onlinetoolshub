Fuel Cost Calculator

Calculate the total fuel cost for any trip based on distance, vehicle mileage and fuel price.

What is Fuel Cost Calculation?

Fuel cost calculation helps you estimate how much you will spend on fuel for a journey. Whether you are planning a road trip, calculating daily commute expenses, or comparing the running cost of different vehicles, knowing the fuel cost in advance helps you budget better. The calculation is straightforward and uses three simple values: distance, vehicle mileage, and fuel price.

How the Fuel Cost Formula Works

The formula used by this calculator is:

Fuel Needed (litres) = Distance (km) / Mileage (km/litre)

Total Fuel Cost = Fuel Needed x Fuel Price (per litre)

Cost per km = Total Fuel Cost / Distance

For example, if you are travelling 500 km in a car that gives 15 km/litre mileage and the fuel costs Rs 105 per litre: Fuel Needed = 500/15 = 33.33 litres. Total Cost = 33.33 x 105 = Rs 3,500. Cost per km = 3,500/500 = Rs 7.

Tips to Improve Vehicle Mileage

Getting better mileage directly reduces your fuel costs. Here are some practical tips:

1. Maintain a steady speed and avoid sudden acceleration or braking.

2. Keep your tyres properly inflated to the recommended pressure.

3. Service your vehicle regularly, including air filter and spark plug replacement.

4. Reduce unnecessary weight in the vehicle.

5. Use the air conditioner judiciously, as it increases fuel consumption.

6. Plan your route to avoid traffic congestion and unnecessary detours.

Petrol vs Diesel: A Quick Comparison

Diesel vehicles generally offer 20-30% better mileage than their petrol counterparts, which can offset the slightly higher per-litre cost in some regions. However, diesel vehicles often have a higher upfront cost. For high-mileage drivers (those driving more than 1,500 km per month), diesel vehicles can be more economical in the long run. For low-mileage city driving, petrol vehicles are usually the better choice due to lower purchase and maintenance costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find my vehicle's mileage?

You can find the claimed mileage in your vehicle's specifications sheet or owner's manual. For real-world mileage, fill your tank completely, note the odometer reading, drive normally, fill the tank again, and divide the kilometres driven by the litres filled. This gives you the actual km/litre figure.

Does city driving give different mileage than highway driving?

Yes, highway driving typically gives 15-30% better mileage than city driving due to constant speeds and fewer stops. If your trip involves both city and highway driving, use an average of both mileage figures for a more accurate estimate.

Can I use this calculator for electric vehicles?

This calculator is designed for fuel-based vehicles. For electric vehicles, you would need to know the energy consumption (kWh per km) and electricity cost (per kWh) instead. The principle is similar, but the units differ.
