What is EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment)?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment, which is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender on a specified date each month. EMIs are used to pay off both the principal and interest on a loan over a set period. In India, EMI-based repayment is standard for home loans, car loans, personal loans and education loans offered by banks and NBFCs.

How to Use This EMI Calculator

1. Enter the total loan amount in INR.

2. Enter the annual interest rate offered by your lender.

3. Enter the loan tenure in years.

4. Click "Calculate EMI" to see your monthly EMI, total payment and total interest payable.

EMI Formula

The EMI is calculated using the reducing balance method with the formula: EMI = [P x r x (1+r)n] / [(1+r)n - 1], where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate / 12 / 100), and n is the total number of monthly instalments. In the early months, a larger portion of the EMI goes towards interest, and over time, a larger portion goes towards principal repayment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the reducing balance method?

In the reducing balance method, interest is calculated on the outstanding loan balance each month, not on the original loan amount. As you repay the principal through EMIs, the interest component decreases over time. This is the standard method used by most banks in India for home and personal loans.

How can I reduce my EMI?

You can reduce your EMI by choosing a longer loan tenure, negotiating a lower interest rate, making a larger down payment, or transferring your loan to a bank offering better rates (balance transfer). However, a longer tenure means you pay more total interest over the life of the loan.

Does prepayment of loan reduce EMI?

Yes, making a part-prepayment reduces the outstanding principal, which can either reduce your EMI amount or shorten the loan tenure depending on the option you choose. As per RBI guidelines, floating-rate home loans in India cannot have prepayment penalties.
