Inflation Calculator

Calculate the future cost of goods after inflation and see how your purchasing power reduces over time.

What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises over time, eroding the purchasing power of money. When inflation increases, each unit of currency buys fewer goods and services than it did before. In India, inflation is measured using the Consumer Price Index (CPI) and has historically averaged around 5% to 7% per year.

How to Use This Inflation Calculator

Enter the current amount of money you want to evaluate, the expected annual inflation rate, and the number of years into the future. The calculator will show you two key results:

  • Future Cost: How much the same goods or services will cost in the future due to inflation.
  • Reduced Purchasing Power: How much your current money will be worth in real terms after accounting for inflation.

Formula

The formulas used in this calculator are:

Future Cost = Current Amount x (1 + Inflation Rate / 100) ^ Years

Purchasing Power = Current Amount / (1 + Inflation Rate / 100) ^ Years

For example, if you have ₹1,00,000 today and inflation is 6% per year for 10 years, the future cost of equivalent goods will be ₹1,00,000 x (1.06)^10 = ₹1,79,085. Conversely, your ₹1,00,000 today will only have the purchasing power of ₹55,839 in 10 years.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good inflation rate to use for India?

The Reserve Bank of India targets an inflation rate of 4% with a tolerance band of +/- 2%. For long-term financial planning, using 6% to 7% is a safe and realistic assumption based on historical averages.

How does inflation affect my savings?

If your savings earn a return lower than the inflation rate, your money loses real value over time. For instance, if your savings account gives 4% interest but inflation is 6%, your real return is negative 2% per year.

Can I use this calculator for retirement planning?

Yes. This calculator helps you understand how much money you will need in the future to maintain your current lifestyle. It is an essential tool for estimating retirement corpus requirements.
